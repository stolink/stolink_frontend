Chapter 1: The Train Station

The steam hissed violently, obscuring the platform in a veil of industrial fog. It smelled of coal dust and damp wool, a scent that Elena would forever associate with leaving. She tightened her grip on the leather handle of her suitcase, her knuckles turning white against the worn brown material.

A conductor blew his whistle somewhere in the distance—a sharp, piercing shriek that cut through the low rumble of the idling engine. People pushed past her, shadows in the mist, their faces turned down or hidden behind newspapers. Nobody looked at anybody else. It was a city of ghosts before the war had even started.

"Ticket, miss?"

She started, turning to see a man in a navy uniform looming beside her. His cap was pulled low, casting his eyes in shadow, but the brass buttons on his coat caught the dull light of the gas lamps overhead.

"Yes," she stammered, fumbling with her free hand in the pocket of her trench coat. "Just a moment." Her fingers brushed against the cold metal of the locket—a small comfort, or perhaps a warning. She wasn't sure which anymore.

The train gave a lurch, metal groaning against metal. The clock on the wall showed 11:58 PM. Two minutes to midnight. Two minutes until the borders closed.
